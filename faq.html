FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is Campaign Cover Charge?

Campaign Cover Charge is a public health campaign meant to promote mask usage among bars and restaurants in the Gainesville area. The campaign was created through the collaboration of The City of Gainesville and The University of Florida.

Are the health promotion behaviors promoted by the campaign mandated?

No. Behaviors promoted by the campaign are guided by CDC recommendations and are, therefore, highly encouraged, but not mandatory or required by law. However, a pandemic is a dynamic situation and you should stay up-to-date with local and state guidelines.

Which Gainesville area bars participate in Campaign Cover Charge?

Some of our most popular partners can be found on the About page, but we are adding partners all the time. Follow us on social media using the links at the bottom of the page for partner announcements!

I want to help! How can I get involved in the campaign?

Well, you're in luck because we hire Campaign Cover Charge ambassadors regularly! Be on the look out for application announcements on social media.

How can bar owners partner with the campaign?

Please reach out to our small business owner liason, Christina. She's awesome!

Do public health professionals advise the campaign?

Absolutely! Experts within UF's College of Public Health and College of Medicine advise our health messaging in addition to CDC guidelines.

Do campaign partners require patrons to be vaccinated?

No, our campaign and partners respect vaccination as a personal choice. While our campaign health adivisors strongly suggest vaccination for the groups suggested by the CDC, the campaign's focus is on masking as a mitigation strategy.
